# Cover Letter Templates and Guide

**Authoritative source:** your university career center guide (`documents/career-guide.pdf`). Pages covering cover letters are the spec. Rules below apply alongside it.

## When to Write a Cover Letter

Check before generating. Generate a cover letter only when:
- (a) the application form has a cover letter upload or free-text field, OR
- (b) the posting explicitly asks for one, OR
- (c) the user requests one.

Many portals (Lever, some Greenhouse forms) have no cover letter field at all. In that case, skip it.

---

## House Style: Same Header as the CV

The cover letter header must be identical to the CV header for a cohesive brand:

```
[Full Name]
[City, State]  //  [email]  //  [phone]  //  [linkedin URL]  //  [github URL]
```

Then: date, company name, company address (optional), salutation.

---

## Format Rules (NCA Standard)

- **Length:** 1 page, 3-5 paragraphs
- **Salutation:** use named recruiter if known; otherwise "Recruiting Team" or "Hiring Manager." Avoid "To whom it may concern."
- **No contact info in signature block** (it's already in the header)
- **Business letter format:** date, company address, salutation, body, closing, signature
- **Same font and sizing as CV** (Times New Roman, 11pt body)

---

## Paragraph Structure

### Paragraph 1 — Opening
- State the role you're applying for (1 sentence)
- Connect your background to the role (1-2 sentences)
- Make it specific to this company/role, not a template opener

### Paragraph 2 — Most Relevant Experience
- Lead with the single most relevant experience for this role
- Quantify the outcome (a metric, a deliverable, a result)
- Frame it as evidence of what you can do for them, not just what you've done

### Paragraph 3 — Why This Company
- Show you've researched the company: a specific product, initiative, market position, or team
- Explain why this company specifically, not just "a company like yours"
- Connect your domain knowledge or perspective to their business context
- If you spoke with someone there, reference it naturally ("After speaking with [name]...")

### Paragraph 4 — Additional Strength (optional, if space allows)
- A second relevant experience or angle not covered in Paragraph 2
- Often useful to show cross-functional or leadership breadth

### Paragraph 5 — Closing
- Brief, confident, forward-looking
- "I look forward to discussing..." or "I would welcome the opportunity to speak..."
- Single thank-you line; no over-enthusiasm or begging

## Tailoring Checklist

Before finalizing a cover letter:

- [ ] Uses the same header as the CV (name, contact line)
- [ ] Salutation names a specific person, or "Recruiting Team" / "Hiring Manager"
- [ ] Opening paragraph is specific to THIS company and role (not templated)
- [ ] All company-specific claims verified via WebFetch/WebSearch
- [ ] At least one quantified outcome in the body
- [ ] "Why this company" paragraph references something specific and real about the company
- [ ] Closing is confident and brief, not apologetic
- [ ] No em-dashes anywhere
- [ ] No personal pronouns in any CV bullets referenced
- [ ] Tone consistent with the CV
- [ ] Exactly 1 page when compiled to PDF

---

## Common Pitfalls

- **Repeating the CV:** the cover letter adds context and forward-looking framing; it does not list every bullet from your resume.
- **Generic opener:** "I am applying to [Company] because I am passionate about technology" — this could be for any company. Be specific.
- **Weak closing:** "Please feel free to contact me" is passive. Use "I look forward to discussing how I can contribute to [specific team or goal]."
- **Clichés:** cut "I believe I would be a great fit," "leverage my skills," "hit the ground running," "I am passionate about," "synergies."
- **Em-dashes:** replace every em-dash with a comma, period, or sentence restructure.

---

## Running the Humanizer

After drafting the cover letter, run the **humanizer** skill on it. The humanizer removes AI-writing patterns (em dash overuse, rule of three, vague attributions, passive voice, filler phrases) and makes the letter sound like a sharp human wrote it. This step is mandatory before the review gate.
